## Multiplicity Distribution Analysis in High-Energy \( e^+e^- \) Annihilation

This repository contains numerical and statistical analyses of charged-particle multiplicity distributions in high-energy \( e^+e^- \) annihilation within the framework of the **Gluon Dominance Model (GDM)**.

The work was carried out as part of the **INTEREST Programme** at the **Joint Institute for Nuclear Research (JINR), Dubna, Russia**, under the supervision of **Prof. Elena Kokoulina**.

---

### Research Objectives

- Study quark–gluon cascade dynamics in high-energy particle interactions
- Analyze charged-particle multiplicity distributions in \( e^+e^- \) annihilation
- Compare theoretical GDM predictions with experimental datasets
- Investigate correlation effects and deviations from Poisson behavior
- Examine energy dependence in multiparticle production processes

---

### Collision Energies Analyzed

Multiplicity distributions were analyzed for the following center-of-mass energies:

- **14 GeV**
- **22 GeV**
- **34 GeV**
- **43 GeV**

---

### Methods and Computational Framework

The analysis was performed using:

- **C++**
- **CERN ROOT**
- **MINUIT-based \( \chi^2 \) fitting**
- Statistical fitting and numerical analysis techniques
- Experimental-theoretical distribution comparison workflows
